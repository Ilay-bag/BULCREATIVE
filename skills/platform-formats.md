# SKILL — Platform Formats (specs, safe zones, native look)

Knowledge base for designing/generating creatives that feel NATIVE to each ad platform.
When a PLATFORM is specified, apply its spec strictly.

## meta-feed (Facebook/Instagram feed)
- Aspect: **1:1** (1080×1080) or 4:5. Hero centered, generous negative space.
- On-image text: minimal and large — the ad is seen at phone size. Headline ≤ ~6 words.
- Copy limits (for any accompanying text): primary text hook must land in the FIRST
  125 characters; headline ≤ 40 chars; description ≤ 30 chars.
- Look: bright, thumb-stopping contrast; product-first.

## story (Instagram/Facebook Stories, vertical)
- Aspect: **9:16** (1080×1920), full-bleed.
- **Safe zones: keep the top ~15% and bottom ~20% free of text and key elements**
  (platform UI: profile bar on top, CTA/swipe area at bottom).
- Vertical composition: hero in the middle band, headline above it, CTA pill in the
  lower-middle (not bottom edge).
- Look: immersive, casual, native — like a story a person posted, not a billboard.

## tiktok (vertical video-frame aesthetics for statics)
- Aspect: **9:16**. Same safe zones as story, plus keep the right edge clear (action rail).
- Look: raw, energetic, UGC-flavored; avoid over-polished studio feel. Bold simple type.
- Ad text ≤ 80 chars.

## linkedin
- Aspect: **2:1** (1200×627 ≈ 1.91:1; 2:1 is the closest generator ratio).
- Look: professional, clean, credible; muted-premium palette; benefit-led headline.
- Intro text ≤ 150 chars recommended; headline ≤ 70.

## free (no platform)
- Aspect: keep the source creative's ratio (or 1:1 for new creatives).
- No extra constraints beyond the design fundamentals.

## Universal rules
- One focal point; the eye path is hero → headline → CTA.
- Design for the 3-second scroll test: the message must land instantly at thumbnail size.
- Match the platform's native energy — a TikTok ad that looks like a LinkedIn banner dies.
